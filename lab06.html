Lab 6 Reflection

My design inspiration for this map was The Masters, specifically a beautiful image of the Augusta National golf course on an early spring day. To me, this represents home. The Masters is one of my favorite Georgia traditions and a place that I love to visit. In addition, my favorite season is spring, especially at home, and the picture of Augusta National that I referenced perfectly captured what I imagine when I think of spring at home. The greens of the course, combined with blooming pink and purple flowers and blue sky, created a calm and welcoming visual mood that I wanted my map to reflect. This led me to use a simple variety of colors from the image including greens, pinks, purples, and some blue. My goal was to create a map that felt clean, peaceful, and visually balanced, similar to the atmosphere of Augusta National itself.

An initial challenge I faced when designing the map at multiple scales was not being able to represent all of the colors that I wanted to. I wanted to create a visually appealing map without it feeling overwhelming. Eventually I realized that it was best to keep colors simple on a large scale then add more on the smaller scale. For example, when zoomed out, the map appears mostly green to maintain clarity, but when zoomed into a city, users can see a greater variety of pink and purple elements. This approach helped maintain readability while still reflecting the original design inspiration.

One technical challenge I encountered was adding icons to the map pins. Not only did I not know how to do it at first, but once I did, I still could not find all the icons that I would have liked due to limited options. At first, I did not even know how to implement them, and even after learning the process, I had a difficult time finding icons that matched my locations due to limited options. To overcome this, I used the AI chat feature in VS Code to help me understand where to implement the code for icons. I also used it to identify available icon options and determine which ones best matched my locations. This process helped me better understand both the coding structure and the design decisions involved in creating an interactive map.

Overall, this project helped me better understand how design inspiration, cartographic decision-making, and technical skills work together to create an interactive map that is both effective and visually appealing.
